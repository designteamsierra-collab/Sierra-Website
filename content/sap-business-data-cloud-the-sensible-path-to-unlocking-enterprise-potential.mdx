In this guide, we will explore how SAP Business Data Cloud transforms data management and how partnering with Sierra Digital can accelerate your journey toward a smarter, more agile enterprise.

<h2>Three Hard Truths About Enterprise Data Today</h2>

Before exploring what SAP BDC can do, it helps to name the specific obstacles that keep enterprise data from delivering its full potential. In our work with SAP customers across oil & gas, manufacturing, and specialty chemicals, three challenges come up again and again.

<h3>Challenge 1:</h3>

<h3>Your Data Is Ready — But You’re Not Allowed to Use It Yet</h3>

Most enterprise data transformation programs are held hostage by their own prerequisites. Before a business team can get a single insight, IT must complete a data migration. Before the migration begins, a governance model must be approved. Before governance is approved, a data dictionary must be built. The result: months or years of runway cost before the business sees any value.

<p>
  SAP BDC eliminates this bottleneck through its “zero-copy” architecture. Rather than requiring you
  to physically move and restructure data before you can use it, BDC connects directly to your
  existing SAP systems — ECC, S/4HANA, BW — and makes their data immediately available as governed
  data products. Sierra Digital’s BDC Horizon program is built around this principle: using
  pre-built content, automated BW/BOBJ/BPC lineage mapping, and a standardized four-step delivery
  method, Sierra clients routinely realize value from BDC within weeks, not quarters. As Sierra’s
  assessment toolkit,{" "}
  <a href="/infocus/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">
    InFocus
  </a>
  , delivers a full migration framework in just five days — covering sources, modelling objects,
  ETLs, reports, and migration plans — there is no blank-sheet discovery, no long prerequisite
  runway, and no manual rediscovery effort.
</p>

<h3>Challenge 2:</h3>

<h3>Your Most Valuable Insights Live Across Systems You Can’t Easily Connect</h3>

<p>
  Some of the most important questions a business needs to answer cannot be answered from a single
  system. Consider a CFO asking: “
  <em>
    What are the fully loaded costs of my workforce, including benefits, overhead, and project
    allocations?
  </em>
  ” The answer requires combining HR data from Workday with financial data from SAP S/4HANA — two
  platforms that have historically never spoken to each other in a governed, semantically consistent
  way. Or consider a supply chain leader who needs to understand vendor risk by correlating
  procurement data in SAP with contract performance data in Salesforce.
</p>

<p>
  SAP BDC is purpose-built for exactly these cross-platform scenarios. Its knowledge graph preserves
  business semantics and context as data flows across systems, meaning that when Workday headcount
  data meets SAP cost center data, the platform understands what both mean and can merge them into
  coherent, trustworthy insights. Sierra Digital has developed a suite of Custom Data Products that
  put this capability to work immediately: Workday Insights delivers fully loaded workforce cost
  analysis with 90% accuracy; Salesforce Insights enables pipeline-to-financials reconciliation with
  an 85% improvement in sales forecast accuracy; and ServiceNow Insights connects incident and asset
  data to financial impact across cost centers. These are not one-off dashboards — they are
  governed, repeatable data products built on BDC’s open data ecosystem, designed to span your
  entire enterprise application landscape.
</p>

<h3>Challenge 3:</h3>

<h3>You’ve Already Invested in a Data Platform — and You Shouldn’t Have to Walk Away from It</h3>

Many enterprises have made significant investments in modern data platforms: Databricks for advanced machine learning and AI/ML workloads, Snowflake or Google BigQuery for cloud-native warehousing, Confluent for real-time data streaming, or DataRobot for automated ML. These are not throwaway investments — they represent years of engineering work, trained models, and business logic. Yet, traditional approaches to SAP data transformation often treat these platforms as competitors rather than complements, forcing organizations to choose between their SAP data and their broader data ecosystem.

SAP BDC takes the opposite approach. Through its open data ecosystem and native Databricks integration — including bi-directional Delta Sharing and Zero Copy federation — BDC acts as a governed bridge between your SAP data products and the broader analytics infrastructure you have already built. Your Databricks AI/ML models gain access to SAP’s rich, semantically governed business data. Your Snowflake or BigQuery workloads can be enriched with real-time SAP operational data without duplicating it. Sierra Digital’s BDC-to-Databricks Delta Share Optimizer accelerates this integration further, enabling enterprises to activate SAP data inside their existing ML pipelines with minimal engineering effort. The result is not a platform replacement — it is a platform multiplier.
